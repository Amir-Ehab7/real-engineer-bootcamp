Executive Summary

In the traditional academic ecosystem, a graduation project is often treated as the final hurdle to obtain a degree. However, in the modern competitive landscape, a graduation project is an engineer's first real-world portfolio.


"To Be a Real Engineer" is a premium program designed to fundamentally shift how engineering students approach, execute, and leverage their graduation projects. By bridging the gap between textbook theories, industrial gaps, and global research standards, this event equips students with the exact mindset, tools, and validation methods required to step into the market as high-caliber, practical engineers.

The Bootcamp Program

Track 01

The Mindset

90 Minutes (Core + Q&A)
  • Market Gaps: Identify real-world industrial bottlenecks & design commercially viable solutions.
  • Team Dynamics: Data-driven criteria for team selection and avoiding toxic group dynamics.
  • Academic vs. Commercial: Structural differences separating an academic project from a scalable product.
Track 02

The Research Track

90 Minutes (Core + Q&A)
  • The Research Flip: Methods to transform your Literature Review into a peer-reviewed Research Paper.
  • Global Mobility: Leveraging projects to secure fully-funded international Master's & PhD scholarships.
  • Academic Writing: Best practices for writing scientific papers & pitching to funding bodies.
Track 03

The Panel Discussion

90 Minutes (Panel + Q&A)
  • Real-World Success Stories: Deep dive into projects that successfully changed lives.
  • Overcoming Failures: Strategies on overcoming early-stage roadblocks & securing seed funding or grants.
Track 04

Tools & Validation

90 Minutes (Core + Q&A)
  • Industrial Simulation: The role of software (Aspen HYSYS, Aspen Plus, MATLAB, ANSYS) in proving feasibility.
  • Validation Mechanics: Cross-verifying data to soundly convince investors and academic juries.
